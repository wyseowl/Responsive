How do I save money?

The only part of the student loan you have control over are the additional repayments. Additional repayments are voluntary repayments you make on top of your mandatory repayments, and this is how you can save money.

Use the charts below to see how much you will save or lose by making additional repayments. You should use the APR to compare how much you are saving with how much you have spent. The higher the APR the better.

Keep in mind that what you spend on additional repayments can be put into a savings account or in other investments which may give a better return. See here for other options.

Visit the Student Loans Company website to see how to make additional repayments.

See how much you should spend on additional repayments

This is the most important chart! Why is it important? You may have saved money by making additional repayments, but you also might have spent a lot.

Your aim is to save a lot compared to how much you spend. This is where APR comes in, as the higher the APR, the more worth it is to make additional repayments.

Before you make additional repayments, you should compare the APR to other savings account interest rates. It may even be worth putting your money into other savings schemes.
