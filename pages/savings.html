Alternative Savings Options



If you're looking to increase your savings but your APR for paying off your student loan was low, you may want to consider other options.

Below you will find a number of alternative options.

Cryptocurrencies:

Bitcoin, Ethereum and Litecoin are types of a revolutionary new set of currencies called cryptocurrencies. These are performing very well at the moment, with the potential for huge profits to be made.

Don't know how or where to buy cryptocurrencies? Sign-up free with the largest cryptocurrency exchange, Coinbase, using this link and receive £7 free if you decide to make a purchase.

Risk: Medium to High

Return: Medium to High



Stocks, Shares and Bonds:

If you don't mind taking on a bit of risk, you can make great savings by investing in stocks, shares and bonds.

Risk: Low to Medium

Return: Low to Medium



Savings Account:

Shop around at banks for the best savings accounts.

Risk: Low

Return: Low



Other Loans:

If you have other loans with higher interest rates than your student loan, it will likely be worth paying those off first. Student loans are quite unique since they are cancelled after a number of years, unlike most other loans, making them even more difficult to make savings on.
